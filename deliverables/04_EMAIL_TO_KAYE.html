Rob,

Working through the Camp Schwab FY26 portfolio (SCH-1024, SCH-3213, SCH-3237, SCH-3270, SCH-3314), I went directly into the PAX 1391 Processor System and reviewed how Block 9 is coded today against what the May 2024 MCON/MCNR Consistency Review Board Guidelines and DD 1391 Development manual prescribes for line-item structure. SCH-1024 is the worked example; the other four follow the same approach.

Two notes up front:

1. The proposed change is in draft only. Nothing has been entered or modified in PAX yet. We are in a pencils-down / editing moratorium for the FY26 cycle.

2. Dollar values do not change. Total Funded Cost and Total Project Cost remain consistent with the cost estimate workbook to the dollar.

What the SCH-1024 PAX entry looks like today, verified by direct review of the Block 9 Data screen, the Associated Costs dialog, and the printed FY 2026 Special Projects Program form (Bil Hawkins Branch Chief approval 09 Apr 2026):

The Block 9 Data items table contains 18 rows: one iNFADS-locked building row at the top, eleven scope rows entered as dollar items with UNIFORMAT II Level 2 prefixes (B20, C10, C30, D20, D30, D40, D50, E20, F20, G10, G20), and six cost-rollup adder rows entered as additional dollar items (Contingency $552K, LSH $2,413K, Planning and Design $509K, SIOH $679K, CM $339K, DB Design $401K). All four percent slots in the Associated Costs dialog (Contingency, Supervision Inspection and Overhead, Design Build, Planning and Design) are set to 0.00%. The printed form rollup section therefore shows Subtotal $10,412K, Contingency (0.0%) $0, SIOH (0.0%) $0, Total Funded Cost $10,412K, Total Project Cost $10,412K, Planning and Design (0%) (NON ADD) ($0).

Proposed coding change for SCH-1024, mirrored across the other four buildings:

1. Remove the eleven UNIFORMAT prefixes from the scope rows and rewrite the descriptions in plain English. Dollar values unchanged.

2. Move the six cost-rollup adders out of the items table as dollar items and enter them through the Associated Costs dialog as percentages: Contingency 10.00%, Supervision Inspection and Overhead 8.00%, Design Build 4.00%, Planning and Design 10.00% (the Planning and Design slot covers the NAVFAC 6% Design plus 4% Construction Management combined; PAX has no native CM percent slot, so CM rides under Planning and Design with the breakout disclosed in the Block 10 narrative).

3. LSH is a Marine Corps FSRM allowance (2.5% of iNFADS PRV per NAVFAC 11010.44E CH-1 / MCO 11000.12) and PAX has no native LSH percent slot in Associated Costs. Two options preserve the dollar value: (a) retain LSH as a single dollar line item in the items table, or (b) bake the LSH amount into the scope total. Both are workable; the methodology call goes to Bil before PAX entry.

After the change, the items table will contain scope only (the iNFADS-locked building row plus the eleven renamed scope rows, optionally plus the LSH line per the methodology call), and the printed form will render Contingency, SIOH, Planning and Design, and Design Build as percent rollup rows below the Subtotal rather than as discrete line items.

Verification step at PAX entry: the exact Total Project Cost on the printed form after the change cannot be predicted without rendering, because PAX's internal base for each percent slot has not been tested outside the current all-zero state. The workbook target Total Project Cost is $10,413K rounded ($10,012K Total Funded). After entry, if a delta exists, resolution is one of: adjust the LSH option, adjust the percentage rates to match PAX's base, or adjust the workbook to match PAX's methodology. The chosen reconciliation gets documented in Section F-11 of the cost estimate workbook.

If the proposed coding reads cleanly, I will mirror the same approach across SCH-3270, SCH-3213, SCH-3237, and SCH-3314 in PAX as soon as the moratorium lifts.
