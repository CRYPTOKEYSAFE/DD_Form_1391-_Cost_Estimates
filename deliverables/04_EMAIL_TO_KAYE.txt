How PAX codes Block 9. There are two operator-controlled inputs and one rendered output, and they live in different places. The Block 9 Data screen holds the items table; columns are Item, Classification of Work, Work Type, UM, Quantity, Unit Cost, and Cost ($000). The first row is reserved for the iNFADS-supplied building line and pulls Description, Classification, Work Type, UM, and Quantity from the property record; that row is not operator-editable. Below it the operator adds rows, either as dollar items (a fixed dollar amount) or as percent items (a rate that PAX computes against an internal base). The Associated Costs dialog opens from a button on the same screen and holds four named percent fields: Contingency, Supervision Inspection and Overhead, Design Build (required), and Planning and Design. Each field accepts a percentage. PAX renders the printed FY 2026 Special Projects Program form from the items table plus the Associated Costs dialog. The items list fills the line-item section near the top of the form. The Subtotal at the bottom of that section equals the sum of the items table rows. Below the Subtotal, PAX prints rollup rows for Contingency, SIOH, Total Funded Cost, Total Project Cost, and Planning and Design (NON ADD), each row showing its rate from Associated Costs and the dollar amount PAX computed.

What is in PAX today for SCH-1024. The Block 9 Data items table holds 18 rows. The top row is the iNFADS-locked building line (MULTI PURPOSE BEQ/BOQ/CO HQS, Quantity 1.00, Unit Cost $10,412,000.00). Below it sit 11 scope rows entered as dollar items, each starting with a UNIFORMAT II Level 2 prefix (B20, C10, C30, D20, D30, D40, D50, E20, F20, G10, G20). The last 6 rows are the cost rollup adders, also entered as dollar items: Contingency $552K, LSH $2,413K, Planning and Design $509K, SIOH $679K, CM $339K, and DB Design $401K. All four percent slots in the Associated Costs dialog are set to 0.00 percent. Because every adder is already absorbed as a dollar line item and the percent slots are zero, the printed form shows Subtotal $10,412K equal to Total Funded Cost equal to Total Project Cost. The math is internally consistent; the question is just where each piece lives in the data model.

What the May 2024 CRB Guidelines say. Section 3.1 sequences Block 9 line items by facility and function name. The word UNIFORMAT does not appear anywhere in the 93-page document. Sections 3.3, 3.4, and 3.5 present Contingency, SIOH, and DB Design as percent rollup lines below the items list, which is the same structure the AF exemplars use and which the PAX 1391 Processor supports through the Associated Costs dialog. The Guidelines do not address LSH, the 6 percent NAVFAC Design, or the 4 percent Construction Management, since those are FSRM Marine Corps program adders outside the MCON / MCNR scope of the document. The local rates are documented in the cost estimate workbooks at Section F-11.

Proposed coding change for SCH-1024, mirrored across the other four when ready. Strip the UNIFORMAT prefixes from the 11 scope rows and rewrite the descriptions in plain English. Move Contingency 10.00 percent, SIOH 8.00 percent, Design Build 4.00 percent, and Planning and Design 10.00 percent into the Associated Costs dialog. The 10 percent Planning and Design covers the NAVFAC 6 percent Design plus the 4 percent CM combined, since PAX has no native CM percent slot; the breakout is disclosed in the Block 10 narrative. Dollar values do not change. The BEFORE / AFTER diagram shows it side by side.

LSH note. The 2.5 percent of PRV LSH allowance ($2,413K for SCH-1024) is a required component of the project. The Guidelines are silent on Block 9 placement, and PAX has no LSH percent slot in Associated Costs. Two options preserve the dollar value. Keep LSH as one dollar line item in the items table (the current PAX entry method). Or fold the LSH amount into the scope total, with the breakout disclosed in Block 10. I would like to walk through this with the team before we settle on the depiction.
